India's Structural Reforms - Part 3

We looked at structural reforms focused on the changing definitions of MSMEs, stressed loans, equity infusion, global tenders made local, e-market technology in India and schemes for NBFCs and power distribution companies in the previous posts.

In this post, we will look at the reforms in the Rural Sector and the emphasis on boosting growth in rural and semi-urban areas. The Government of India has taken steps to address issues faced by small farmers, migrant workers and small workers.

  1. Kisan Credit Cards
    In order to strengthen the farming sector, around 3 crore farmers are likely to get Rs 2 lakh crore of concessional credit line through Kisan Credit Cards. This will help farmers gain access to institutional credit at concessional interest rate. This, in turn, will boost the agriculture sector.

  2.  Nation with Ration
    The concept of One Nation One Ration Card is being introduced by Government of India. People can buy ration at any ration depot in the country. This is primarily to help the poor migrant labourers who face a lot of challenges.
  3. Low Rental Housing
    During the lockdown, many migrant workers struggled to get basic necessities and faced uncertainty about their employment. The government has announced Low Rental Housing Schemes to especially help these migrant workers/urban poor workers
    Government funded Housing will be converted into Low Rental Housing Complexes through the Public-Private Partnership (PPP) model. This will help the urban poor and migrant labourers to have basic accomodation.
  6. Reforming MGNREGS
    The Government of India under this scheme, has announced additional rs.40,000 crore allocation for the rural employment guarantee scheme. This will help in providing jobs to migrant workers who are returning home. By this, the Government aims at de-urbanisation, so that people in Rural India will get job opportunities in their region itself.
  7. Additional Working Capital Funding of Rs.30 Crores by NABARD
    Through National Bank for Agriculture and Rural Development, additional money will be given to small and marginal farmers. This amount will go for the pre-kharif (monsoon crop) preparation and also post-rabi (winter crop) harvesting.

This marks the end of the series of blogs on India's Structural Reforms.
