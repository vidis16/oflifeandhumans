India's Structural Reforms - Part 2

We looked at structural reforms focused on the changing definitions of MSMEs, stressed loans, equity infusion, global tenders made local and e-market technology in India in the previous post.

In this post, we will look at special liquidity scheme and partial guarantee scheme regarding NBFCs and liquidity injection to power distribution companies.
  1. Special Liquidity Scheme- Rs.30,000 Crores for stressed Non Banking Finance Companies (NBFC’s)
    Sectors which have been severely affected on account of Corona Virus are NBFC’s, Real Estate, Infrastructure and Power Distribution Companies.  

    Non Banking Finance Companies (NBFCs) get funding/money from Banks. Banks who get money from RBI at the repo rate lends to NBFCs at higher rates. NBFCs, in turn, lend the money to sectors where bank do not lending i.e. sectors like Real Estate, Infrastructure and Power Distribution Companies. Due to COVID-19, these sectors have been badly hit because of which NBFCs are under severe stress. 

    Government of India has come out with Special Liquidity Scheme of 30,000 Crores to help the stressed NBFC’s.  
    A Special Purpose Vehicle (SPV) will issue bonds guaranteed by the Government of India and this will be purchased by RBI. The money will be used to acquire certain debts of investment grade of eligible NBFCs/Housing Finance Companies. 

  2. Partial Guarantee Scheme
    45,000 Crores Rupees will be given as partial guarantee by the Government of India for very low rated NBFC’s/Housing Finance Companies and Micro Finance Institutions. 

  3.  Liquidity Injection of 90,000 Crores to Power Distribution Companies
    Government of India announced liquidity injection of Rs.90,000 Crores to the stressed power distribution companies.  
    The power distribution companies payable to power generation and transmission companies were approximately Rs.94,000 Crores.  

    Due to the lockdown in the country, commercial spaces like malls, factories, industries have been shut. Therefore, the demand for power has been low. These power distribution companies are, thus,experiecing a cash crunch.

    Power Finance Corporation and Rural Electrification Corporation will provide liquidity worth Rs.90,000 crore to these companies and loans would be given against state guarantees, to ease the pressure faced by these companies.
