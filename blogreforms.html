India's Structual Reforms

In today’s world, many economies are taking heavy loans on account of which their debts are increasing. Hence Debt to GDP ratio has multiplied. On account of high debts, borrowing costs are increasing and margins are becoming thinner.
Economies that expand debt either too quickly or too much, eventually face increased risks thereby leading to higher borrowing costs. High Borrowing costs generally tend to slow down the growth.
The adverse shock of Corona Virus is majorly a combination of supply-demand shock and financial shock.
Government of India has taken very smart steps at Micro Levels:
  1. Changing definitions or Amendments to classification of Micro, Small and Medium Enterprises (MSMEs)  
    By this, the intention is to bring many small companies into the MSME category and get the benefits given to MSME’s. Turnover of less than 100 crores and very small companies will come under MSME segment.
  2. Collateral Free Loans of 3 Lakh Crore
    Around 45 Lakh MSME Companies will benefit through this scheme. Ideally, loans are offered by financial institutions like Banks/NBFCs to MSMEs. The loan shall be given to MSME companies whose turnover is less than 100 crores and having loan outstanding up to Rs.25 Crores. Credit Line up to 20% of the entire outstanding credit may be sanctioned. No collateral security or guarantee required. Loan tenure will be for 4 years and in the first year, no principal repayment nor any EMI’s to be given i.e. moratorium of 12 months on principal repayment. This will help in increasing the confidence and growth of MSME’s. 
  3.  Stressed Loans of 20,000 Crores 
    There are lot of stressed assets and assets which are Non-Performing Assets and MSMEs that have NPAs/stressed assets will be eligible for this scheme. About 2 Lakh MSMEs will benefit from this scheme and there will be Partial Guarantee by the Government. This could help in revival of the existing loans. On account of the Corona Virus issues, these MSMEs have been finding it very difficult to get access to funds. 
  4.  Equity Infusion Fund –50,000 Crores
    Currently many MSME’s are facing shortage of funds. There are quite a lot of MSME’s which are currently not performing well or having shortage of funds on account of the Corona issue. Government is identifying those companies which are likely to do well and have a good growth potential. Government will be providing equity funding for these MSME’s. By this, it will help the companies to expand size as well as capacity and also encourage them to get listed on stock exchange board. 
  5.  Global Tenders Made Local
    Those goods and services valued less than 200 crores to be procured from domestic firms. In case of Government Procurement, tenders will no longer be open for Global Companies. This in fact will be open for domestic MSME’s only. By this move, it will help Indian Companies to bid for government projects like procuring telecom equipment, electronic PBX systems, fibre optic cable, Wi-Fi routers and modems, items for army/military canteen supply etc which are less than 200 crores. 
  6. E-Market -Technology India
    With E-Market Technology (Digitalization), MSME companies are exhibit their products at International Level. The requirement for physical exhibition doesn’t arise. By digitalization, MSME companies will be able to sell or market their products online.
