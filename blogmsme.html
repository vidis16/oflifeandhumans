What are MSMEs?

23rd May 2020

Recent Amendments to make more companies fall under MSME Segment
The Government of India has decided to revise the criteria of Micro, Small and Medium Enterprises (MSME segment). As per the revised definitions, any firm which has investment up to Rs.1 crore and with turnover under Rs.5 Crores will be under ‘Micro’ Enterprise.
A Company which has investments up to Rs. 10 crores and turnover up to Rs. 50 crores will be classified as ‘Small’ Enterprise.
A Firm which has investments up to Rs. 20 Crores and turn over under Rs.100 Crores will be classified as ‘Medium’ Enterprise.
The basic idea is that more firms or companies come under the MSME criteria and can get the benefits what the government has laid down in the stimulus package.
MSME contribution to India’s exports has been around 48% and plans to raise it to 60%. Likely, the contribution to the GDP is likely to increase to 50% from the current 29%. These revisions are also likely to give a push for the “Make in India” motto. When foreign companies step into India, the ancillary manufacturing units can be taken over by these MSMEs. This can help in improving the Local Area/Rural Area Growth thereby resulting in job creations in the near future.
